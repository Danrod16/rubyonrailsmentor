What Counts as a Support Ticket?

To ensure you get the most out of your subscription, it's important to understand what constitutes a support ticket. Remember, the goal is for you to learn and grow, not for me to build the app for you. Here's how we define a support ticket:

Examples of Suitable Support Tickets

  • Guidance on how to implement a user profile page with basic details
  • Help with debugging a form validation issue
  • Advice on setting up email notifications for user actions
  • Tips for designing an admin dashboard layout
  • Assistance with integrating a third-party API

Not Suitable for a Single Support Ticket

  • Requesting a complete messaging system with chat rooms to be built (this would require multiple tickets)
  • Asking for an entire e-commerce checkout flow to be developed (this would be multiple tickets)
  • Developing a complex reporting dashboard with multiple charts (this would require several tickets)
  • Building a complete membership system with different access levels (this would be multiple tickets)

Don't worry!I'll help you break down your project into manageable support tickets that align with your learning goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Rails mentorship service.

What kind of support can I expect?

You'll receive personalized support through our ticket system and scheduled calls, tailored to your chosen plan. This includes assistance with debugging, code reviews, architectural guidance, best practices advice, and career development support. We ensure detailed explanations with each response, helping you understand both the solution and the reasoning behind it.

How quickly will I get responses to my questions?

For the Basic plan, expect responses within 12 hours during business days. Advanced and Premium plan members receive priority support with even faster response times. All responses are thoughtful and detailed, focusing on solving your immediate problem and enhancing your learning experience.

What skill level is required to start?

The mentorship is suitable for all skill levels, from beginners who've just completed a bootcamp to intermediate developers looking to level up. We'll adapt the guidance and explanations to match your current understanding and help you progress at your own pace. The key is having a basic understanding of Ruby and a willingness to learn.

What topics are covered in the mentorship?

We cover all aspects of Ruby on Rails development, including: Ruby fundamentals, Rails conventions and best practices, database design and ActiveRecord, testing strategies, API development, deployment and DevOps basics, code optimization, and security best practices. We also address career development topics like interview preparation and portfolio building.

How are the mentorship calls structured?

Mentorship calls (available in Advanced and Premium plans) are focused sessions where we can dive deep into your code, do pair programming, or discuss broader career topics. You can use these calls for code reviews, architecture planning, debugging sessions, or career guidance. The agenda is flexible and driven by your needs.

Can I change or cancel my subscription?

Yes! You can upgrade, downgrade, or cancel your subscription at any time. There are no long-term commitments required. If you need to pause your mentorship for any reason, you can do so and resume when you're ready. All plans are billed monthly, and you can make changes before your next billing cycle.

How do I get started?

Getting started is easy! Choose your preferred plan and book a free introduction call. During this call, we'll discuss your goals, assess your current skill level, and create a personalized learning plan. After that, you'll get immediate access to the support system and can start submitting your questions and scheduling calls (depending on your plan).
